THE LOT SIZING PROBLEM

The LSP consists of finding, for each product and each period of the timeline,
the production rate needed to satisfy the demand while minimising the overall cost,
which includes both fixed (e.g. set-up) and variable per-unit costs (e.g. production, stock).

Variables:
- x_jt: amount of product j produced in period t
- y_jt: set-up variable. Equals 1 if x_jt > 0, 0 otherwise
- s_jt: stock of product j in period t

Parameters:
- d_jt: demand of product j in period t
- c_jt: unitary cost of production for product j in period t
- f_jt: unitary cost of stocking for product j in period t
- g_jt: unitary cost of set-up for product j in period t
- K: max per-product production capacity in every interval
- R: max overall production capacity in every interval
- Q: max overall stock capacity in every interval

Model:
min  sum_{j,t} c_jt * x_jt + sum_{j,t} f_jt * s_jt + sum_{j,t} g_jt * y_jt

subject to:
(balance) x_jt + s_j(t-1) -s_jt =  D_jt         for all j, t
(stock_capacity) sum_j s_jt <= Q                        for all t
(prod_capacity_K) x_jt - K * y_jt <= 0                       for all j, t
(prod_capacity_R) sum_j x_jt <= R                        for all t
(non_negativity) s_jt >= 0, x_jt >= 0                  for all j, t
(binary) y_jt in {0, 1}                         for all j, t
